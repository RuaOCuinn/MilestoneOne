About - Oscar Pearson

The Early Years

Oscar Pearson was born in Dublin, Ireland to wonderful parents who encouraged him to read and create through reading to him, gentle boosts of self-esteem, and the removal of all televisions from the house. During his early years, he read extensively and wrote awful short stories to teach himself what not to do.

Oscar went to the University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point, initially studying civil engineering which led to a revelation that civil engineering is boring. He then spent the next nine years jumping from course to course, taking time off, enjoying semesters at part-time, and generally enjoying the college student experience before being kindly asked to graduate already. Surprisingly enough, he had enough credits to graduate with an English major, and he did so grudgingly.

From Man-child to Author

All this time Oscar was working on “The Book,” as he and his friends lovingly titled it. When he returned to Balbriggan he began teaching half-time while trying to sell The Book to publishers. In the process, he disguised a chapter of The Book as a short story and won the Writers of Tomorrow competition in 2013. This put him into contact with all the right people, and after deciding to split The Book into three installments, DAW agreed to publish it. In March 2014, Bloodstone: Book One was published to great acclaim, winning the Quill Award and making the New York Times Bestseller list.

All this success was wonderful. Patrick eventually had to stop teaching in order to focus on writing, though he messed that up by having a very cute baby with his very cute girlfriend. He started a charity fundraiser called Bookers and published a scary children’s book called The Adventures of the bear and Mrs. whopper in July of 2016 through Hobnob Press.

After a lot of hard work and a few stern emails from his patient editor, Bloodstone: Book Two came out in March 2019 to even more acclaim, making #1 on the New York Times Bestseller list. Life continues to go pretty well him, and he’s working hard on writing the final installment of the series.
